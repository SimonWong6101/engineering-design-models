Rough Machining with Localized Clean-Up

For components with multiple pockets and machining areas, machining sequences can be defined to rough the pockets with a relatively large tool and then secondary sequences can be defined to clean up the remaining material.


The area of material to be removed is easily identified. This component is a support bracket with internal ribs. The area of interest is the internal material around the ribs.

The first sequence is intended to remove material as quickly as possible, therefore the tool used is required to be larger than the smallest radius in the area being machined.

This results in a compromise in that this will leave material in these areas. In order to deal with this situation local milling sequences can very easily be generated which will automatically clean up the areas left by the initial sequence. In this case the material left is calculated from the previous tool path

Pro/MFG enables detailed control of the sequences. For example it is possible to control which of the corners are machined with each individual sequence, and also the order in which they will be cut. This will help to ensure that the sequences generated are as efficient as possible.

Depending on how rough the initial volume sequence needs to be it may be required to nest more than one local milling sequence. This is achieved very easily simply by referring to the previous local sequence and selecting a smaller tool.


It is also often necessary to control the order in which different segments of the main volume cut are performed. Initially here we are simply asking the system to machine neighboring areas one after the other.

As the ribs are relatively thin here, perhaps a better way to handle the initial material clearance would be by machining all pockets together at each height pass. This will help to prevent distortion of ribs when machining an area with no material supporting it.
