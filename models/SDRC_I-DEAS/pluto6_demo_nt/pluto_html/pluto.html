The Pluto Express Satellite Demo
A study in the use of CAE tools for Analysis
 
 
The study of the reflector dish sub-assembly (the dish and the support ribs) was the first step in our analysis of the satellite geometry.  We wanted to look at and analyze the design to determine what problems might be inherent to the design, and optimize the design for known operating conditions.

Normal Modes, and Optimization on the Reflector Dish
 
For further component analysis, we needed to utilize the geometry from our suppliers and our design team :
  • Legacy or External geometry
  • Section meshing - Geometry simplification and/or abstractions
  • Meshing techniques - Automatic meshing (free and mapped), Manual meshing
  • Laminates
  • Beams
  • Assembly meshing
The review of the design for the Pluto Express Satellite indicated a number of analysis types that needed to be performed in order to verify the design proposed.  These Included:
  • Nonlinear Analysis on the Fuel Tanks
  • Thermal on the bus and reflector for internal and external (orbital) conditions
  • Linear Statics on the bus and reflector using the thermal results
  • Electronic Systems Cooling on the testing equipment to insure proper performance
  • Durability on specific components in the positioning jets
  • Dynamics on the entire assembly
  • External solvers for verification / company requirements
